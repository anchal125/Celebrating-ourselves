Breathing is essential part of any yoga pose which are related to Pranayama or Meditation.
Sukhasana too works best when the inhalation and the exhalation is done with awareness of the movement of the spine.

1.Inhale: Bring your legs close to you, cross them at the shins, and place the feet below the opposite thighs.
2.Exhale: Once the legs are in this position, exhale.
3.Inhale: Place the outer wrists on the closest knee, with the thumb and the index finger joined at the tips.
4. Have the other three fingers stretched out and pointing downwards.
5.Exhale: Raise the back and align the hips such that you are sitting on the sit bones.
6.Inhale: Close your eyes, loosen the body and relax the eyes completely.
7.Exhale: Raise the entire upper body upwards and bring the chest out in front.
8. Pull in the tummy to the maximum extent possible without feeling any stress while doing it.
9. Inhale and Exhale: Begin the slow movement of the body and with every inhalation bring the body loose and as you exhale expand the spine upwards and move upwards pushing the lower back inside and upper back upwards. Sink the legs and the knees deep into the floor without pushing them. Repeat this and slowly bring the body and mind awareness to the Pranayama you are practicing while in this yoga pose and relax your facial muscles.
